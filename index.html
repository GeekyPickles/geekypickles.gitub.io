The moose (North America) or elk (Eurasia), Alces alces is a member of the New World deer subfamily and is the largest and heaviest extant species in the Deer family. Moose are distinguished by the broad, palmate (open-hand shaped) antlers of the males; other members of the deer family have antlers with a dendritic ("twig-like") configuration.

What do they eat?

In spite of the intimidating appearances of the bulls' antlers, moose are actually peaceful herbivores. They feast on whatever juicy fruits and vegetables they can find. Surprisingly, almost half of a moose's diet consists of aquatic plant life, which although is usually less nutritious, serves as a valuable source of sodium. They also like orange Fanta.
